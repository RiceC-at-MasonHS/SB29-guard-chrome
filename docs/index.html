Privacy Policy for the "SB29 Guard" Extension

Last Updated: November 24, 2025

The SB29 Guard Chrome Extension is designed with a "Privacy First" architecture. Our mission is to help students and teachers identify approved software without compromising their own privacy.

Data Collection Policy: Zero Data Collection

This extension does not collect, store, or transmit any personal user data to external servers. It operates entirely as a "client-side" application.

Why does the extension ask to "read my browsing history"?

Transparency Notice: When installing, Chrome may warn you that this extension can "read and change all your data on the websites you visit" or "read your browsing history."

We require this permission solely to provide the automatic "Guard" feature. Here is exactly how it is used:

  • The Function: The extension automatically changes its icon color (Green, Red, Yellow) when you navigate to a website that matches your district's software list.
  • The Technical Requirement: To perform this check automatically in the background, the extension requires the host_permissions (specifically <all_urls>) to detect the URL of your active tab.
  • The Privacy Guarantee: This URL check happens 100% locally on your device. The extension compares the URL you visit against a list stored locally in the extension's memory. It does not send your browsing history to a cloud server, database, or third-party analytics service.

How It Works

  1. District Data: You or your administrator provides a link to a public CSV file containing approved and requested software.
  2. Local Storage: This list is downloaded and stored locally within the extension's storage on your computer.
  3. Local Checks: When you visit a website, the extension checks the URL against the list it has stored locally. Your browsing history, the sites you visit, and any other personal data **never leave your computer**.

Information We Explicitly DO NOT Collect

We want to be perfectly clear. The "SB29 Guard" extension does not have access to, nor does it collect, any of the following for analytics or transmission:

  • Your browsing history log
  • Your name, email address, or any other personally identifiable information (PII)
  • Data you enter into forms on websites (passwords, search terms, etc.)
  • Your IP address

Changes to This Policy

If we ever update this policy, we will reflect the changes on this page. The core principle of zero data collection will not change.
